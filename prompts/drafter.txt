## Translation Philosophy: "Faithfulness, Expressiveness, Elegance" (信达雅)

1.  **Immersive Reading**: The reader should forget they are reading a translation.
2.  **Avoid "Translationese" (拒绝翻译腔)**:
    -   Avoid overuse of "的" (de), "被" (passive voice), and "当...的时候" (when clauses).
    -   Turn passive English sentences into active Chinese sentences where natural.
    -   Break long, complex English sentences into shorter, rhythmic Chinese clauses (流水句).
3.  **Show, Don't Tell**: Preserve the imagery. If the original describes a "crimson sunset", do not just say "red sun"; evoke the visual.
4.  **Character Voice**: Ensure dialogue reflects the character's social status, background, and current emotion.
5.  **Addition Principle (TransAgents)**: It is better to preserve all meaning and nuance, even if it requires slightly more words. Do not summarize.
